The Chef's Story

Where Passion Meets the Palate

Chef Jean-Luc Moreau's journey began in the sun-drenched hills of Provence, where his grandmother's farmhouse kitchen first ignited a lifelong love affair with food. After training at Le Cordon Bleu and earning his stripes in Michelin-starred kitchens across Europe, he returned to his roots to create something truly his own.

At La Maison Dorée, every dish tells a story — of terroir, of tradition reimagined, of ingredients sourced from the finest purveyors who share our commitment to excellence. The menu changes with the seasons, a living document of Chef Moreau's unending curiosity and respect for the craft.

"Cooking is not about perfection," Chef reflects. "It is about connection — to the land, to the ingredient, to the guest seated at your table."

— Jean-Luc Moreau, Chef & Founder

Cellar Selection

The Wine List

'18 Château Margaux Bordeaux, France
$280 +

A masterful vintage from one of Bordeaux's premier estates. Deep ruby in color, with aromas of blackcurrant, violets, and cedar. Silky tannins lead to an extraordinarily long finish.

Pairs beautifully with the Herb‑Crusted Rack of Lamb.

'16 Opus One Napa Valley, California
$350 +

A legendary Napa Cabernet Sauvignon blend. Layers of blackberry, cassis, and dark chocolate with hints of tobacco and vanilla. Full-bodied with polished, integrated tannins.

Ideal alongside the Pan‑Seared Duck Breast.

'19 Gaja Barbaresco Piedmont, Italy
$220 +

Angelo Gaja's iconic Nebbiolo. A fragrant bouquet of rose, cherry, and spice with earthy undertones. Elegant structure with refined tannins and remarkable depth.

A classic match for the Wild Mushroom Risotto.

'20 Cloudy Bay Sauvignon Blanc Marlborough, New Zealand
$95 +

The benchmark Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc. Vibrant notes of passionfruit, citrus, and fresh-cut herbs. Crisp acidity with a lingering mineral finish.

Excellent with the Seared Scallops.

'15 Dom Pérignon Rosé Champagne, France
$320 +

An extraordinary rosé Champagne. Delicate notes of wild strawberry, pomegranate, and brioche with a persistent, creamy mousse. A wine of grace and power.

A sublime pairing with the Foie Gras Terrine.
